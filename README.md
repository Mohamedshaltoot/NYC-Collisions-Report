## 📖 Project Overview
<p align="justify">
This professional analytics project provides an in-depth exploration of New York City traffic collision data from 2021 to 2023. By leveraging advanced data modeling, the dashboard uncovers critical patterns in road safety, helping stakeholders identify high-risk periods, major contributing factors, and the demographic impact of vehicular incidents across all NYC boroughs.

## 🚀 Key Strategic Insights (KPIs)
<p align="justify">
The analysis focuses on five core pillars of traffic safety metrics:
</p>

* **📉 Incident Trends:** A comprehensive overview of **63,751 total collisions**, highlighting a significant improvement in safety by 2023.
* **⚠️ Risk Factors:** Identification of **"Failure to Yield Right-of-Way"** as the most dangerous driver behavior (63.7% danger rate).
* **🕒 Temporal Patterns:** Data reveals peak accident frequency during the **Spring** season and on **Fridays** during the evening rush (4 PM - 8 PM).
* **🏥 Casualty Analysis:** A granular breakdown of **31,376 injuries** and **161 fatalities**, categorized by motorists, pedestrians, and cyclists.
* **📍 Geospatial Impact:** Filtering by location (Bronx, Brooklyn, Manhattan, Queens, Staten Island) to pinpoint regional safety needs.

---

## 🛠️ Technical Implementation
* **Data Architecture:** Processed via **Excel VBA** and Power Query for clean, structured datasets.
* **Analytical Engine:** Built with **DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)** to calculate Year-over-Year (YoY) growth and dangerous percentages.
* **Visualization:** Modern UI/UX design in **Power BI**, featuring custom slicers, heatmaps, and trend area charts.
